With the recent passing of the North American
Free Trade Agreement and the establishment
of the World Trade Organization, serious questions have been raised concerning the effects
of global trade on sustainability, which includes
not only a healthy economic base, but also a
sound environment, stable employment, adequate purchasing power, distributional equity,
national self-reliance, and maintenance of cultural integrity. Subject explores the many
dimensions of sustainability and the use of
political and legal mechanisms to further sustainable development.
