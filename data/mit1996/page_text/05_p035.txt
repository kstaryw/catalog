17.205 Health Policy
Prereq.. -
U (Spring)
3-0-9 HASS
Analyzes the health policy problems facing
America including adequate access to care,
the control of health care costs, and the encouragement of medical advances. Considers
market and regulatory alternatives as well as
possible foreign models including Canadian,
Swedish, Japanese, Britisn, and German arrangements. Emphasis on historical development,
interest group behavior, and organizational influences in setting and implementing policy.
H. M. Sapolsky
17.207 Race and Science
Prereq.: -
Acad Year 1996-97: U (Spring)
Acad Year 1997-98: Not offered
3-0-9 HASS
Examines comparatively the historical and contemporary role of science in constructing
knowledge about human differences in terms
of race. Particular attention is paid to the social
and political context of scientific inquiry and to
its political consequences for public policy.
Considers the role of science in constructing
national identities. Topics include: various national immigration policies, international
eugenics movement, Nazi science, I.Q. and
educational testing.
M. Nobles

(Subject meets with 17.253)
Prereq.: 17.241
Acad Year 1996-97: Not offered
Acad Year 1997-98: G (Spring)
3-0-9 H-LEVEL Grad Credit
Analysis of contemporary and historical issues
in US political economy, with special emphasis
on public finance and economic regulation.
Selected topics in trade and tax policy, agricultural, transport, labor, and environmental
regulations, and local public finance treated in
some detail. Focuses primarily on the neoclassical/rational-choice approach; strengths and
weaknesses of this approach are discussed.
Where necessary, devotes time to developing
tools used in rigorous microeconomic analysis.
Graduate students are expected to pursue the
subject in greater depth through reading and
individual research.
