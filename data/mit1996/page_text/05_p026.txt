16.343 Spacecraft and Aircraft Sensors and
Instrumentation
Prereq.: 16.040, 8.03
Acad Year 1996-97: Not offered
Acad Year 1997-98: G (Fall)
3-0-9 H-LEVEL Grad Credit
Covers fundamental sensor and instrumentation principles in the context of systems
designed for space or atmospheric flight. Systems discussed are incoherent and doppler
radars; space communications; spacecraft attitude determination by stellar, solar, and
horizon sensing; remote sensing by
radiometry, spectrometry, and interferometry;
air data systems; and radio navigation. Also,
review of basic electromagnetic theory and antenna design. Discussion of design considerations for flight.

planetary exploration. Topics: celestial

recursive algorithms for space navigation.
